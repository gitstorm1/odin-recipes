Fried Chicken

Description

Chicken Fried Chicken is a beloved dish that stands as a testament to the simple joy of home cooking. This dish starts with a boneless, skinless chicken breast that's been marinated in a medley of spices, infusing it with a burst of flavor that permeates every bite. The chicken is then dredged in a seasoned flour mixture, creating a coating that will transform into a crispy, golden crust upon frying. The result is a piece of chicken that's wonderfully crunchy on the outside, yet remains incredibly tender and juicy on the inside.

The magic of Chicken Fried Chicken doesn't stop at the chicken itself. It's traditionally served with a generous ladle of creamy, white gravy that adds a layer of richness to the dish. The gravy, often speckled with bits of pan drippings, complements the savory chicken perfectly, making each bite a symphony of flavors. Accompanied by classic sides like mashed potatoes or coleslaw, Chicken Fried Chicken is more than just a meal - it's a heartwarming experience that brings the comfort of home to your table. Whether you're enjoying it at a family dinner or a casual lunch, Chicken Fried Chicken is a dish that's sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • 30 saltine crackers
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons dry potato flakes
  • 1 teaspoon seasoned salt
  • Â½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 6 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Place crackers in a large resealable plastic bag; seal bag and crush crackers with a rolling pin until they are coarse crumbs. Add the flour, potato flakes, seasoned salt, and pepper and mix well.
  2. Beat egg in a shallow dish or bowl. One by one, dredge chicken pieces in egg, then place in bag with crumb mixture. Seal bag and shake to coat.
  3. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. Fry chicken, turning frequently, until golden brown and juices run clear, 15 to 20 minutes.
